The blockchain revolution

Feb, 2022 / by Sebastian Pardo

Blockchain is a revolutionary technology invented in 2009, it was originally developed as a method to record cryptocurrency transactions; however, the functionality of blockchain has evolved in different applications such as financial markets, accounting, government insurance and others.

Blockchain technology is a distributed database, where each user in the network executes and records each transaction by grouping them in the form of blocks. It is a safe and transparent way to store information. Blockchain has the ability to record all kinds of transactions, the information is verifiable and immutable; this technology can verify the authenticity of any type of information, finishing with many crimes such as theft and digital corruption.


This technology was implemented in some companies that wanted to advance in the new data security technology, blockchain offers advantages such as exchanging data without the need for third parties, transparency, effectiveness in their transactions, and simplifies the company's accounting system. The change generates a great impact on the way business has been carried out, and opens the possibility of new ways of doing business.

However, blockchain has some disadvantages, being a relatively new technology, some aspects of the use of information must still be standardized, in the same way, that it is considered a more secure technology than conventional ones, does not make it unbreakable to any attack.

Finally, it can be concluded that technology is constantly changing, the revolution of cryptocurrencies, NFTs and the metaverse is only just beginning, and we must be flexible in order to adapt and know how to use the technologies that are yet to be discovered.
